Streaming Setup Issues – Technical Troubleshooting Guide

This page focuses on technical fixes for common streaming app setup issues: app not loading, login failures, buffering, and device conflicts.

For the full Peacock TV–focused help page, visit the main documentation hub: https://DigitalMediaPlatformSupport.github.io/

1. App opens but keeps buffering

  1. Run a speed test on your connection (you generally want 10 Mbps+ for HD streaming).
  2. Restart your router and modem, then restart the streaming device.
  3. Close all other high-bandwidth apps on the same network (downloads, torrents, cloud backups).
  4. Lower video quality in the app settings (Auto → 720p or similar).
  5. If you use Wi-Fi, test by connecting the device via Ethernet or moving it closer to the router.

2. App not loading or crashing on start

  1. Force close the app and reopen it.
  2. Clear the app cache (on Android/Android TV) or reinstall the app (on Fire TV / Roku / Smart TV).
  3. Ensure the device OS is updated to the latest version available.
  4. Disable VPN/Proxy temporarily and test again.
  5. Test another streaming app to see if the issue is app-specific or device-wide.

3. Sign-in and account issues

  1. Confirm your email and password work on the streaming service website.
  2. If two-factor authentication is enabled, complete that step on another device and retry.
  3. Remove the device from the account’s “logged-in devices” list (if the provider offers it), then sign in again.
  4. Check date/time settings on your TV, phone, or streaming stick (incorrect time can break tokens).
